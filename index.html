Description

Boast is a card based role-playing game with an emphasis on storytelling. Players take on the role of an old pirate, boasting about their past exploits. They tell tales about their legendary journeys. At the end of each round, players award the best stories with beer tokens. Cards are used to inspire player stories and encourage group interactions. The game takes only an hour to play and uses no dice or complicated guide books and is dm-less. 
                                                                                                                        

History

The game started off as  one of Charlie's assignments in USCâ€™s Game Design Workshop class. It was focused on evoking a specific emotion, and Charlie eventually onboarded Kai and a few others onto the team. The pirate theme  resonated with Charlie's long standing love of all things pirate, and his passion has been driving the project ever since.

Throughout the development process it was important to to keep the gameplay contained to a single, quick and impactful session because Charlie hadn't ever actually finished a rpg campaign from start to finish. He would always meet with his friends for their annual game night only to end up never finishing the campaign. Boast has been heavily distilled over play centric design methods to bring out a short, fun and highly replayable role-playing experience.

About the Team

Charlie Dunlap
Charlie is a computer science games major at University of Southern California focused on game design and engineering. He is a 34 year old U.S, Army veteran and the Creator of Boast. In addition to Boast he is the co-creator and engineer of Compromised. Outside of game creation he enjoys cosplay and is a motorcycle enthusiast.

Kai Nyame Drayton-Yee
Kai is a game designer and current first year at the University of Southern California studying Interactive Entertainment. She is 18, and the head designer of multiple projects including the physical field game Ideal Meal and leads the Gameheads youth game development program. In her free time, she enjoys crafting cosplay and practicing capoeira.

Nicholas Roush
Nicholas is an 18-year old student, currently studying game design at the University of Southern California. He is originally from a lower middle-class family in Omaha, Nebraska. He believes in spreading ideas through games and new media to make the world a smarter and more cooperative place. Currently, he is working as the producer for the field game Ideal Meal, along with other projects.
